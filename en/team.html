artistic direction

Carmen G. Jara
musical composition

Musical studies at the Professional Conservatory of Albacete and at the “Oscar Esplá” Superior Conservatory of Alicante. Later she continued her postgraduate studies at L'Scola de Música de Barcelona, ​​at the University of Barcelona and at the University of Málaga, obtaining her doctorate in 2012.


She completed her musical training in various national and international piano improvement, accompaniment, improvisation and composition courses with teachers: Albert Atenelle, Walid Alk, Emilio Molina, Almudena Cano, Aquiles de la Vigne, Leonel Morales, Doru Stephan, José Felipe Díaz, John Salmon, Solomon Mikowksky


In the 2013/2014 academic year she moved to Amsterdam with a scholarship from the Dutch Nationale Opera and Ballet of Amsterdam. And she continued her specialization with the pianists Olga Khoziainova, Olga Smirnova and Rex Lobo.


As a pianist she has been part of different chamber groups, both instrumental and vocal, performing recitals throughout Spain and participating in Festivals such as Musicosophia (Friburg), International Chamber Music Festival (Valencia), Terral, Malaga Film Festival, etc.


In recent years, linked to the world of dance, she has accompanied classes and courses from the National Ballet of Spain, Dutch National Ballet, Junior Company of Dutch National Ballet, Dutch National Ballet Academy (NBA), Las Palmas Choreographic Center and teachers such as Ana Baselga, Antonio Ruz, Carolina Márquez, Marnie Thomas, Ernst Meisner, Rinat Gizatulin, Guillaume Graffin, Anatole Yanowsky


In 2015 she created the multidisciplinary training AtanosS Project together with the dancer Ricardo Ocaña (Spanish dance).


Her role as a composer is linked to dance, theater and the audiovisual genre. She has written, among others, the scores for the plays: Ay, Carmela! (E.S.A.D. 2010) and Blanco Weiss (2021); the soundtracks of video creations and short films such as: B (Biznaga de Plata, Noche en Blanco of the 2012), A Happy Woman, (2019) Mk2 Short Fest Award, etc. She has composed the music for the dance shows, Tú y Yo by DA.TE Danza and Desiring Machines by Colectivo La Ballesta.


In 2009 she was nominated for the V Jerry Goldsmith Music Awards for the original soundtrack of Life Flows. And in 2014 she was awarded the First Women and Culture Composition Prize of the Provincial Council of Malaga for the work Los Sueños de Orexis o de Deseo.


She is an accompanying pianist at the Conservatorio Superior de Danza de Málaga and directs C-Art Dance.

Carolina Márquez
choreography

Graduated in Graham technique and Pedagogy at the Martha Graham School in New York under the direction of Marnie Thomas and Virginie Mecene. Diploma in Professional Dance Studies (Trinity Laban Conservatory, London). Graduated in Contemporary Dance Choreography (Superior Conservatory of Dance, Málaga).


Experience as a pedagogue in different schools in the United States and Spain. New York Theater Ballet, Webster University, Washington University, COCA, San Diego Ballet, Madrid Dance Center, Scaena Carmen Roche, and Conservatorio Superior de Danza de Málaga,among others. In 2023/2024 she was a professor of contemporary dance at the Marbella Conservatory of Dance.


In collaboration with el Curro DT, Carolina organized courses and workshops of Graham Technique(™) in Madrid and Málaga with former Martha Graham´s principal dancers as Marnie Thomas, David Chase, Blakeley White-McGuire, Lloyd Knight. In 2015 she obtained the temporary right to perform the work "Dark Meadow" with the caraBdanza company. In 2017 she organized the first Graham Teacher Workshop in Spain with Marnie Thomas at the Málaga Conservatory of Dance. That same year, Carolina began a collaboration with Techniche di Danza Moderna under the direction of Caterina Rago. With C-Art Dance she organizes various master classes, including with Joyce Herring at the Center for Choreographic Creation of Andalucia in Seville and with Ramón Oller at the Málaga Conservatory.


Throughout her career she has been part of different creative works as a performer and choreographer at Curro DT (Madrid), Laban (London), Trolley Dance Festival (California), Trinity College of Music (London), “From the ground” dance festival (NY), ARCO (Madrid) , COCA (St. Louis), Alto Jonio Dance Festival (Italy), Martz Contemporary Dance Company (Madrid), and RDC Youth Company (Birmingham), among others.


She was co-Director of the caraBdanza company and co-founder of the caraB Performing Arts school in Madrid. Some of her choreographic works are “A solas”, “Khemia”, “After the rain”, “9D56”, “Hiraeth” and “Incision”.


She is the representative in Spain of the platform “Graham for Europe” which promotes the Graham technique in Europe and director of C-Art Dance.

Zaida Ballesteros

She studies at the Granada Professional Conservatory of Dance. She later graduated from the University of Frankfurt in classical and contemporary dance.


She has worked as a dancer with companies such as: Osnabrück State Theatre, St. Gallen State Theatre, Lausanne Opera, Schauspielhaus Stuttgart, Berliner Staatsoper. And choreographers like; Sasha Waltz & Guest (Körper, NoBody & Dialogue), Constanza Macras (Big in Bombay), Iztok Kovac, Dieter Heitkamp, ​​Marco Santi, Melissa King, Toula Limnaos, Vera Sanders, Luc Dunberry, Andrea Boll, Yossy Berg and Oded Graf, Linda Kapetanea and Jozef Fruzek, Philipp Egli, Anton Lachky, Aitana C. Vico. From 2016 to 2020 she teaches classes at the Mozarteum, Carl Orrf Institute, Salzburg. From 2017 to 2022 she directs the dance section of the Piccolo Theater (Cottbus).


She completed her Master's Degree in Theater Research in 2018. She currently works as an interim dance professor at the Conservatorio Superior de Danza de Málaga, and as a choreographer and independent dancer; and writes her Doctoral Thesis.

Carlos Novella

Graduated in Classical Dance from the Professional Conservatory of Seville, he continues his training at the Andalusian Dance Center as a scholarship student in the Neoclassical specialty.


He has danced for Companies such as DA.TE Danza ("Nudos") or Cía. Fernando Hurtado ("El paraíso de los necios", "De flor en flor"). In February 2024 he premiered his first long solo piece "Maricón", a work awarded by MálagaCrea.


He is currently continuing his training in Contemporary Dance Choreography at the Superior Conservatory of Málaga.

Inma Montalvo

Master in Cultural Production and Management at the Open University of Cataluña. Graduated with a double specialty as a Pedagogue and Choreographer in Contemporary Dance at the Superior Conservatory of Dance of Málaga. Postgraduate in Contemporary Dance at the Andalusian Dance Center of Seville.


Since 2008 she has been part of the Fernando Hurtado Dance Company as a dancer in all its shows and as co-director in different national and international projects. At an international level she has carried out projects for: National Ballet of Peru, the National Company of the Dominican Republic, the National Company of Bolivia, the National Company of Salvador.


She is one of the directors of La Danse Professional professional training in Málaga.

Sandra Abril

Graduated in classical dance from the Reina Sofía Professional Dance Conservatory of Granada. Degree in Contemporary Dance Choreography at the Superior Conservatory of Dance of Málaga. Master in Dance from the International University of La Rioja (UNIR).


She has worked with Rafael Amargo Company in Dionisio, Yerma and The Beautiful Dream of Life. Dancer of the Fernando Hurtado Company in Alice and Aires Revueltos. She joins the Merlin Company, participating in international events such as the closing of the Ocean Club Marbella and the Riyadh Theater Festival.


She explores her creative side through the creations of Be Reborn and Mónada. Experience as a teacher in institutions such as the Solange Jassens Private Conservatory of Almuñécar, the Headway Dance Studio School and the Happy Style School.

Carlos L. Camps

Master in Advanced Theater Studies (UNIR), Degree in Contemporary Dance Choreography (CSD Ángel Pericet, Málaga), Contemporary Dance at the Andalusian Dance Center of Seville. He also has training in lighting for live shows and construction of scenery/stage machinery by Escénica Técnicos (Seville).


As a performer/dancer he has worked with different companies such as Cía. Marina Miguélez/Dance Craft, Silencio Danza, Cía. Fernando Hurtado, Cía. Ana Rando, La Pública/Paco Nevado and Producciones Imperdibles, passing through festivals such as TAC in Valladolid, MUT in Castellón, Palma del Ría Theater Fair, Granada Music and Dance Festival, Marbella en Danza, Host Festival among others. Additionally, as a dancer he has participated in operatic productions at the Teatro Cervantes and Teatro de la Maestranza.


In choreography he has developed several short format pieces such as “Portraits” (duo) and Sjnor (duo) together with Haku Guerrero, “Disappear and Ser Body” (solo), Sísyfo (video dance). In stage direction and movement assistantship he has worked with Carolina Márquez in Hiraeth, with Salud Pérez in Palestra, with Jana Pacheco in Ramificación Felinas and with Mª José Villar in Ergot.


He combines stage work with dance classes and choreographic workshops, collaborating with different schools such as Love2Dance (Seville), Escuela de Danza CM (Málaga), Marbella Dance School MDS (Marbella) or Semana de la Danza de Bollullos del Cdo. (Huelva).

Marina Miguélez Lucena

Graduated in classical dance from the Mariemma Royal Professional Conservatory of Dance in Madrid. Higher Degree in Contemporary Dance Choreography at the Higher Conservatory of Dance of Málaga.


She worked as a freelance dancer at (Interdans, Belgium), as a choreographer (Festival Cultural Bad Homburg Frankfurt, Internationales Solo Tanz Theater Festival - Stuttgart), and teaching classical and contemporary dance courses at conservatories and important dance schools in Spain and Europe. From 2008 to 2015 she was part of the Nuremberg State Ballet, where she worked under the artistic direction of Goyo Montero and performed mainly leading roles such as “Clara” in The Nutcracker, “Juliet” in Romeo and Juliet, or “Margarita” in Faust, among others.


In 2014 she received the Bavarian Cultural Prize, awarded by the German ministry, in recognition of her professional career.


She also had the opportunity to dance pieces by other choreographers such as Jiři Killian, Ohad Naharin, Johan Inger, Mats Ek, Nacho Duato and Crystal Pite. She continued as a soloist at the Bayerischer Kunstförderpreis in 2014 and joined the Saarland State Ballet in September 2015, where she also performed leading roles under the direction of Stijn Celis.


She currently runs her company "Dance Craft" and is part of the team of ESAEM teachers in Malaga.
